Abstract

Identities are abstract concepts that represent entities within a given context. They may take various formats and serve diverse purposes. In data ecosystems, in which different participants share data and services in a secure, interoperable, and sovereign manner, identities are primarily considered for identification, authentication, and authorization. Here, identities must be represented concretely and precisely. Digital Identity Description Language (DIDL) is an abstract language to describe identity concepts used in data ecosystems. The core component, identity, is specialized into identifiers, credentials, and assertions, utilized in the process of authentication and authorization.
